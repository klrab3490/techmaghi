US designates The Resistance Front, LeT proxy behind Pahalgam attack, as Foreign Terrorist Organization

In a move underscoring growing US-India counterterrorism cooperation, the United States has designated The Resistance Front (TRF), a proxy of the Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT), as a Foreign Terrorist Organization (FTO). The designation comes in response to TRF’s alleged role in the April 22 attack in Pahalgam that left 26 civilians dead.
Secretary of State Marco Rubio announced the designation on Thursday, stating that it aligns with President Donald Trump’s demand for accountability in the aftermath of the Pahalgam attack.

In a move underscoring growing US-India counterterrorism cooperation, the United States has designated The Resistance Front (TRF), a proxy of the Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT), as a Foreign Terrorist Organization (FTO). The designation comes in response to TRF’s alleged role in the April 22 attack in Pahalgam that left 26 civilians dead.
Secretary of State Marco Rubio announced the designation on Thursday, stating that it aligns with President Donald Trump’s demand for accountability in the aftermath of the Pahalgam attack.
“This action against the TRF demonstrates the Trump administration’s commitment to protecting our national security interests, countering terrorism, and enforcing President Trump’s call for justice for the Pahalgam attack,” Rubio said in a statement issued by the Department of State.
Rubio added that the TRF, along with other aliases, has been added to LeT’s designation as both an FTO and a Specially Designated Global Terrorist (SDGT) under Section 219 of the Immigration and Nationality Act and Executive Order 13224.
Reacting to the news, External Affairs Minister S Jaishankar said: “A strong affirmation of India-US counter-terrorism cooperation.”
